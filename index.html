ABOUT KOOKING IN THE KITCHEN: WOMEN REDESIGNING DESIGN

Attention! Anyone sick of learning about the white male big shots that dominate design industries. Woman-identifying artists tell their stories and address how navigate male-dominated design fields. Sponsored by the KITCHEN KONVERSATIONS podcast, Faith Shakira and Rebecca Loren have created this event for women to claim a rightful place within creative industries. Join us for the first annual Kooking in the Kitchen speaker series.

For more information, feel free to visit KITCHEN KONVERSATIONS on Spotify.

TALKS

2021 TALKS

  • DESIGNING CULTURE 05/20 18:00

    Ji Won Choi and Solange discuss where culture falls within design, and how to navigate intersections between "design culture" and "experiential culture."

  • OTHER THAN THE WHITE MALE IN THE ROOM 05/21 18:00

    Nicole Guerriero and Barbara Kruger talk about clawing to success through white walls, specifically white male walls. The two speakers will be calling audience members onto the stage to workshop personal situations of how to navigate industry.

  • LEGACY 05/22 18:00

    Choi, Guerriero, Knowles, and Kruger join forces one last time for a candid talk of what the future looks like for "othered" creatives. They ponder on how technology should be used for equality advancement in the future, and preach the necessity of population in order to make long-lasting change within industry specifically.

TALK TO US!






    Ji Won Choi is a fashion designer born in South Korea and raised in Oklahoma. Growing up feeling so different, she rejected her Korean culture until she discovered new cultures while living in New York, Paris, and Milan. Traveling the world, she discovered how beautiful each individual culture is and how different crafts, materials, techniques, and silhouettes of any culture can come together to create something impactful that celebrates our differences. The main ideas of her philosophy is to create not only beautiful clothing, but clothing that have meaning. She aims to promote global cultures by bringing different parts of the world together, while being created responsibly and sustainably.

    For more information, please visit jiwchoi.com

  • Barbara Kruger is an American conceptual artist and collagist associated with The Pictures Generation. Most of her work consists of black-and-white photographs, overlaid with declarative captions, stated in white-on-red Futura Bold Oblique or Helvetica Ultra Condensed text.

    For more information, please visit Barbara Kruger's ArtNet page

  • Solange Piaget Knowles is an American singer, songwriter, record producer, performance artist, and actress. Expressing an interest in music from an early age, Knowles had several temporary stints as a backup dancer for Destiny's Child, which featured her elder sister, Beyoncé Knowles, among its members, before signing with her father Mathew Knowles's Music World Entertainment label. At age 16, Knowles released her first studio album Solo Star (2002). Between 2005 and 2007, Knowles had several minor acting roles, including the direct-to-video Bring It On: All or Nothing (2006) and continued writing music for Beyoncé and for former Destiny's Child group members Kelly Rowland and Michelle Williams.

    For more information, please visit Solange on Spotify
