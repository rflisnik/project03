Kooking in the Kitchen: Women Redesigning Design

  • ABOUT

    • Attention! Anyone sick of learning about the white male big shots that dominate design industries. Woman-identifying artists tell their stories and address how navigate male-dominated design fields. Sponsored by the KITCHEN KONVERSATIONS podcast, Faith Shakira and Rebecca Loren have created this event for women to claim a rightful place within creative industries. Join us for the first annual Kooking in the Kitchen speaker series.

  • SPEAKERS
    • JI WON CHOI

      • Ji Won Choi is a fashion designer born in South Korea and raised in Oklahoma. Growing up feeling so different, she rejected her Korean culture until she discovered new cultures while living in New York, Paris, and Milan. Traveling the world, she discovered how beautiful each individual culture is and how different crafts, materials, techniques, and silhouettes of any culture can come together to create something impactful that celebrates our differences.The main ideas of her philosophy is to create not only beautiful clothing, but clothing that have meaning. She aims to promote global cultures by bringing different parts of the world together, while being created responsibly and sustainably.

    • BARBARA KRUGER

      • Barbara Kruger is an American conceptual artist and collagist associated with The Pictures Generation. Most of her work consists of black-and-white photographs, overlaid with declarative captions, stated in white-on-red Futura Bold Oblique or Helvetica Ultra Condensed text.

    • NICOLE GUERRIERO

      • Makeup and beauty guru with an enormous social media following who was named Ryan Seacrest Best Beauty Guru of 2013. She runs a blog called Naturally Nicole where she posts tutorials, DIYs, reviews, and fashion blogs. She worked at a multitude of restaurant jobs, including 5 years working at BJ's Brewhouse, before starting YouTube. She also worked as a bartender.
